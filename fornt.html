Introduction
Sense organs are specialized organs that help to perceive the world around us. They are an integral part of our lives and it is the only way that enables us to perceive the environment. Sense organs provide the required data for interpretation through various organs and a network of nerves in response to a special physical phenomenon. These senses govern our association.

Sensory Receptors:Sensory receptors reacts based on the type of stimuli.

Types of Sensory Receptors:
S.No Sense Receptors Functionality
1 Mechanoreceptors Pressure receptors, stretch receptors, and specialized mechanoreceptors involved in movement and balance.
2 Thermoreceptors Skin and viscera, respond to both external and internal temperature.
3 Pain receptors Stimulated by lack of O2, chemicals released from damaged cells and inflammatory cells.
4 Chemoreceptors Detect changes in levels of O2, CO2, and H+ ions (pH) as well as chemicals that stimulate taste and smell receptors.
5 Photoreceptors Stimulated by light

Eye structure and its Parts

Eyes-Sight or Ophthalmoception These are the visual sensory organ in our body. These are sensitive to light images. The eyes vary in colour depending upon the amount of melanin present in our body. It helps in the sense of sight by detecting and focussing on the light images.

Rods:These sensors function in low light and are found at the edges of the retina. They also aid in peripheral vision.
Cones:hese type of retinal cells work best in bright light, detecting fine details and colour.




Ear structure and its Parts

Ears – Hearing or Audioception Ears are the auditory sense organs of our body. They help us to perceive sounds. Our auditory system detects vibrations in the air and this is how we hear sounds.







Nose structure and its Parts


Nose – Smell or OlfalcoceptionThe nose is an olfactory organ. Our olfactory system helps us to perceive different smells. This sense of organ also aids our sense of taste. The sense of smell is also known as olfaction.






Skin structure and its Parts


Skin – Touch or TactioceptionSkin is the largest organ of our body. The sense of touch is also referred to as tactioception.kin receptors generate an impulse, and when activated, is carried to the spinal cord and then to the brain.





Tongue structure and its Parts



The tongue is a muscular organ in the mouth of most vertebrates that manipulates food for mastication and is used in the act of swallowing.Like fingerprints, your tongue has a unique print pattern and it’s home to 5000 taste buds!









Welcome,Interesting Facts about Sense Organs

Eye

1.Eyes are made up of over 2 million working parts.
2.For a day our eye blinks 25000 times and an average of 4,200,000 times a year.
3.80% of our memories are determined by what we see.
4.The eye is only part the which functions with 100% ability at any moment.

Ear

1.Your outer ear never stops growing throughout your lifetime.
2.The ear contains more than 20,000 hair cells.
3.Of the 206 bones in the human body, the six smallest are in your ears: three in the right and three in the left. Together, each three are about the size of a pencil eraser.
4.The hardest bone: the temporal bone, which protects the inner ear, is the hardest in the human body.

Nose

1.We have over 400 olfactory receptors, making your sense of smell one of the strongest senses.
2.A single sneeze can release 40,000 droplets from our nostrils and mouth. These droplets can travel over five feet!
3.Our nose shapes the sound of our voice.
4.Our nose regulates the temperature of the air we breathe.

Tongue

1.The average adult has between 2,000 to 4,000 taste buds in total.
2.Your tongue is strong and has 8 muscles. Four muscles allow our tongue to change shape and the other four allow your tongue to change position. These muscles move your tongue in different directions and help you speak.
3.Tongue is not the only place where taste buds live. Taste buds can also be found on the inside of our cheeks, on lips, on the roof of our mouth and even under the tongue.

Skin

1.The average person has about 300 million skin cells.
2.Skin accounts for about 15% of your body weight.
3.The color of human skin depends on the amount of pigment melanin that the body produces. Small amounts of melanin result in light skin while large amounts result in dark skin.
